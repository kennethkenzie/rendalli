Twenty-four hours. That's the window between a fish leaving our water and landing on a kitchen table in Kampala. It's not a marketing number — it's a discipline.

## The morning harvest

We start before sunrise. The crew meets on the dock while the lake is still silver. Nets come up, each fish is weighed, checked, and packed into insulated crates with ice crushed that same night.

> Good food starts with good fish. That's our promise to every family.

Speed isn't the point. Freshness is. Speed is just what freshness requires.

## The cold chain

From boat to truck, we never break the cold chain. A single reading above 4°C would compromise everything. So we measure — constantly.

- Crates lined with food-grade insulation
- Temperature loggers in every load
- Direct route to the Bulenga shop, no stopovers

## The shelf

By 11am the fish is in the counter. By dinner, it's on a plate. That's the whole point of Rendalli — not to be clever, but to be close.

Close to the water. Close to the farm. Close to your family.
