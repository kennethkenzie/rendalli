Tilapia is forgiving. It's hard to ruin. But it's even harder to make extraordinary — unless you know these three moves.

## 1. Charcoal grilled — the family table

Score the skin. Rub with salt, lemon, garlic. Grill 4 minutes a side over hot coals. Serve with posho and greens.

This is the one we make on Sundays. It feeds six and asks for nothing but an appetite.

## 2. Pan-fried — the weeknight save

Dry the fillets well. Season, dust with rice flour. Hot pan, hot oil, 2 minutes each side. The rice flour gives you a crust you could call dangerous.

> Fresh Nile Tilapia, farmed with care. Pick yours up today.

## 3. Slow-smoked — the project

Set up an indirect fire with mango wood. Keep the temperature below 100°C. Ninety minutes, skin-on, whole fish.

The result is a smoky, tender, almost-spreadable fillet. Flake it over warm rice, or press it into sandwiches the next day.

---

The secret to all three? **Don't overcook.** Tilapia is done when it just flakes — not when it fights you.
